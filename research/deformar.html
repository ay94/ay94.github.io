The Project

Context & Motivation

Placeholder — describe the research context here. What gap in the literature does DeformAr address? What are the limitations of existing NER evaluation approaches for Arabic?

Existing NER evaluation for Arabic relies heavily on aggregate F1 scores, which obscure systematic failure modes specific to morphologically complex languages. DeformAr was developed to provide a richer, diagnostic picture of model behaviour.

My Role

Research Contributions

Placeholder — describe your specific research contributions. What novel ideas, frameworks, or experiments did you design and lead?

Sole researcher and developer. Conceived the diagnostic framework, designed the error taxonomy, built the extraction and evaluation pipeline, and developed the interactive dashboard for result exploration.

Method

Framework & Approach

Placeholder — describe the technical methodology in detail. How does DeformAr work? What datasets, models, and evaluation protocols were used?

DeformAr introduces an entity-level error taxonomy for Arabic NER, classifying errors into categories such as boundary errors, type confusion, and morphological decomposition failures. Cross-lingual benchmarking protocols compare Arabic model performance against English baselines on parallel corpora.

Outcomes

Findings & Impact

Placeholder — describe the key findings from the research. What did DeformAr reveal about Arabic NER model behaviour? What are the implications for practitioners?

DeformAr surfaces that the majority of Arabic NER errors are boundary-related rather than type errors — a finding obscured by aggregate metrics. The diagnostic dashboard enables practitioners to identify model weaknesses on specific entity types and domains.
