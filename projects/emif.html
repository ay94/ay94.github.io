Institute for Strategic Dialogue (ISD) / European Media and Information Fund (EMIF)

Pro-Kremlin Influence Network Mapping

Published · 2024

The Project

Context & Objectives

A joint research project between ISD and CASM Technology, funded by the European Media and Information Fund. The study investigated how pro-Kremlin, conspiracy, far-right and far-left communities interact on Telegram across France, Germany and Italy — mapping influence networks, identifying key actors, and tracking narrative spread over a two-year period (January 2022 to February 2024).

The findings were published in a public report: "Behind the Curtain: Unveiling Pro-Kremlin Ecosystems in Europe" (ISD, 2025).

My Role

Contributions

Technical Lead. I designed and built the entire analytical pipeline — from data preprocessing through semantic mapping to classification. I also wrote the methodology section of the published report.

The contrastive learning fine-tuning library (emif-pk) was my own independent initiative, built and tested on top of the project data to improve embedding quality across clusters.

Method

Approach & Pipeline

  1. Data collection — 7,784,669 posts from 1,162 Telegram channels across France, Germany and Italy
  2. Preprocessing — deduplication, hashtag/URL/mention removal → 4,910,302 unique messages
  3. Sampling — stratified sampling to 353,778 messages (max 400 per channel)
  4. Embedding — paraphrase-multilingual-mpnet-base-v2 sentence transformer
  5. Dimensionality reduction — UMAP applied to embeddings
  6. Clustering — HDBSCAN density-based clustering → 162 topic clusters
  7. Manual coding — analysts reviewed 100 random samples per cluster → taxonomy of 22 themes and 113 subthemes
  8. Validation — random stratified sample reviewed per subtheme
  9. Classification — KNN classifier applied to full dataset (F1: 0.87)
  10. Contrastive fine-tuning — built emif-pk library using contrastive loss to improve embedding homogeneity across clusters

Outcomes

Results & Impact

Thematic taxonomy of 22 themes and 113 subthemes applied across 4.9M classified posts.

Key finding: 77% of conspiracy channels interacted directly with pro-Kremlin communities.

Published report available at isdglobal.org. Contrastive learning library (emif-pk) built as reusable internal tool, available on request.
