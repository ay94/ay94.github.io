Ofcom

Disinformation Landscape Mapping & Actor Profiling

Pilot · 2023

The Project

Context & Objectives

A pilot project for Ofcom — the UK's communications regulator — to map the disinformation landscape on social media and build detailed behavioural profiles of key actors. Rather than simply identifying what topics exist, the goal was to understand who talks about what, how actors cluster thematically, and what each actor's specific contribution to the disinformation ecosystem looks like.

My Role

Contributions

Technical Lead. I designed the multilayered topic modelling architecture specifically for actor profiling — adapting the framework first developed on the Swedish Institute project to produce account-level thematic fingerprints rather than just corpus-level themes.

Method

Approach & Pipeline

  1. Data collection — curated list of social media accounts identified as disinformation-adjacent actors
  2. Layer 1 topic model — generic BERTopic across full dataset → 5 broad themes (Health, Politics, and others)
  3. Layer 2 topic models — separate BERTopic per theme → granular sub-topics (e.g. Health → COVID, Vaccines, Pharma conspiracy; Politics → Elections, Deep State)
  4. Actor profiling — each account mapped to its dominant themes and sub-topics
  5. Behavioural fingerprinting — accounts characterised by: dominant themes, specific sub-topics, actor interactions, and unique contribution to the ecosystem

Outcomes

Results & Impact

Multilayered disinformation landscape map produced. Account-level thematic profiles built for curated actor list.

Framework demonstrated the viability of moving beyond simple topic classification to behavioural actor profiling. Pilot delivered — further phases pending.
