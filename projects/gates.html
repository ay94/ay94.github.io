Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation (via ITAD)

Global Health Advocacy Evaluation

Completed · 2022

The Project

Context & Objectives

The Gates Foundation commissioned ITAD to evaluate the effectiveness of its Global Fund Advocacy Portfolio (GFAP) — a network of NGO grantees working to influence governments to increase contributions to the Global Fund for AIDS, TB and Malaria ahead of the 7th replenishment.

CASM Technology provided the social media analysis module, collecting and analysing 43,282 posts across Twitter, Facebook and Instagram from grantee accounts in the UK, US, France, EC and International markets.

My Role

Contributions

Technical Lead and sole designer of the thematic framework. I built the social media data pipeline, designed the relevancy classification system (Direct / Indirect / Irrelevant), created the full thematic framework of 18 themes and subthemes, and produced the narrative and tactics analysis per market and per grantee.

ITAD used my analysis as one input layer into their broader mixed-methods evaluation — alongside surveys, interviews and country case studies.

Method

Approach & Pipeline

  1. Data collection — 43,282 posts scraped across Twitter, Facebook and Instagram from grantee accounts
  2. Relevancy classification — three-tier system: Direct (explicitly about Global Fund replenishment), Indirect (related global health content), Irrelevant
  3. Thematic framework design — 18 themes and subthemes including Pledges, COVID-19, Campaign Mobilisation, LMIC Voices, Promoting the Global Fund, Financial Aid, Key Events
  4. Narrative analysis — per market (UK, US, France, EC, International) and per grantee organisation
  5. Tactics breakdown — identifying inside track (direct government engagement) and outside track (public mobilisation) approaches
  6. Synthesis — outputs fed into ITAD's country-level and grantee-level synthesis reports

Outcomes

Results & Impact

43,282 posts analysed across 5 markets. 18-theme framework designed and applied across all grantee accounts.

Narrative and tactics breakdown delivered per grantee and per market. Analysis fed into ITAD's published evaluation for the Gates Foundation.
